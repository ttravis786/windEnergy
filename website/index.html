The Problem

Scotland wastes its greatest asset

Two broken markets — and one solution that fixes both.

Curtailment Crisis

Scotland's wind farms generate far more electricity than the transmission grid can carry south. In 2023, grid operators paid over £700 million to switch turbines off — electricity generated and immediately discarded. Curtailment is growing faster than grid upgrades can address.

910 GWh wasted in 2023 alone

Data Centre Energy Cost

Energy is a data centre's single largest cost — typically 40–60% of operating expenses. UK grid electricity runs at £100–140/MWh. Meanwhile, enterprise customers face mandatory Scope 2 emissions reporting from 2026 but cannot access genuinely renewable compute. Certificate-based green claims are widely criticised as greenwashing.

£100–140 per MWh on the open grid
The Solution

Build for others first. Own the best sites later.

WindEdge's primary model is a build-and-manage service — designing and commissioning wind-adjacent GPU facilities for clients, charging a fixed build fee and ongoing management fee. On-site battery storage bridges wind gaps, keeping GPUs running continuously. As revenue grows, WindEdge builds and owns facilities directly — capturing 100% of revenue at the best sites.

01

Phase 0 — Self-Funded Pilot

Buy 4 × RTX 5090 GPUs (~£2,500 each = ~£10,000 total) and sell compute on Vast.ai and RunPod. No wind farm. No investors. Real revenue data in weeks — then direct contracted relationships with startups, universities, and enterprises replace anonymous marketplace listings.

Bootstrapped
02

Phase 1 — Build & Manage (Primary model)

Design and commission 100 kW modular GPU facilities (~60 GPUs) for wind farm operators, landowners, and private investors. Clients own the hardware. WindEdge charges a fixed build fee plus an ongoing management fee (10% of GPU revenue) to run the EMS algorithm and GPU marketplace. Asset-light — build fees cover operations from day one.

Asset-light
03

Phase 2 — Own the Best Sites (Alternative / long-term)

Once WindEdge has established revenue from the build-and-manage service, the company builds and owns facilities directly at the highest-curtailment Scottish sites — capturing 100% of GPU revenue. Alternatively, a seed raise funds this step sooner. Either path leads to the same outcome: WindEdge-owned facilities running at ~£5–20/MWh versus £100–140/MWh on the grid.

Core IP

The Energy Management System

WindEdge's proprietary EMS is the primary patent candidate — a real-time multi-objective optimisation algorithm controlling physical electrical load switching using probabilistic wind forecasts, live turbine SCADA data, grid electricity spot prices, and battery state of charge as simultaneous inputs. Designed by two CERN physicists who build exactly this class of real-time optimisation system daily.
